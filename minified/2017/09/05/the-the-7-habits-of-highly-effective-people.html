I thought The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People will be similar to The Power of Habit, I was wrong.

I did not plan to read The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People after The Power of Habit, but I’m glad I did because both books complement each other, and to me this one depends on the knowledge you gain from the latter.

There are lot of topics covered that definetly make you look at things from a different perspective:

  • Begin with the end in mind
  • The Circle of Concern
  • The work will come again, but childhood won’t
  • Parapeto Principle

The actual 7 Habits are really useful for properly managing your priorities, having a better personal and work relationships and constant self-improvement. It is a long book but every minute spent reading is well worth it.
